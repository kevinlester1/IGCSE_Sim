USB Architecture

  • Power: Provides +5V power to devices.
  • Serial: Transmits data bit by bit using Data+ and Data- pins.
  • Hot Swappable: Connect/remove while computer is on.

Take your understanding further by investigating these real-world data transmission case studies.

1. The Voyager Probe Dilemma

Voyager 1 is currently over 15 billion miles away from Earth. It takes hours for a signal to reach it.

Research Tasks:

  • Does Voyager use Serial or Parallel transmission? Why?
  • Is the communication Simplex, Half-Duplex, or Full-Duplex?
  • How does NASA deal with data corruption over such vast distances?

2. The Death of Parallel

Old computer motherboards used "Parallel ATA" (PATA) for hard drives. Modern computers use "Serial ATA" (SATA) and PCIe.

Research Tasks:

  • If Parallel sends more bits at once, why did engineers switch to Serial for faster speeds?
  • Investigate the engineering terms Clock Skew and Crosstalk.

3. Undersea Internet Cables

The internet spans the globe through massive fiber-optic cables resting on the ocean floor.

Research Tasks:

  • How do fiber optics achieve Full-Duplex transmission?
  • What is "Wavelength Division Multiplexing" (WDM)?
  • Find a map of submarine cables. What happens if one breaks?

4. The Magic of USB-C

USB Type-C is replacing almost every other cable, handling power, video, and data all at once.

Research Tasks:

  • Look up a pinout diagram for a USB-C connector. How many pins does it have?
  • How does USB-C know which way is "up" when you plug it in?
  • Compare the max speeds of USB 2.0 vs USB4.
