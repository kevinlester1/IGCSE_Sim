Why do we need encryption?

Public Networks are Noisy

Data travels through cables, routers, and Wi-Fi signals where anyone with the right tools can "listen" to the traffic.

Protecting Sensitive Data

Passwords, bank details, and personal messages need a secure envelope to protect them from malicious actors.

Scrambling the Message

Encryption mathematically scrambles data, turning readable Plaintext into unreadable Ciphertext.

✨ The Golden Rule

Encryption is the process of encoding data so it cannot be understood without a specific, mathematical Key to decode it back.

Step 1: The Setup

Bob creates an open padlock (Public Key) and keeps the only key for it (Private Key) safe with him.

How are Asymmetric Keys made? (RSA)

It relies on a mathematical "trapdoor function" involving massive prime numbers.

  • 1. Pick two secret prime numbers (P and Q).
  • 2. Multiply them to get N (The Public Lock). It is easy to multiply.
  • 3. The Trapdoor: It is incredibly hard to figure out P and Q if you only know N. Factoring large numbers takes computers thousands of years.

🎨 The Paint Analogy

Imagine mixing two secret paint colors (Primes) together in a bucket (Public Key). Anyone can see the final mixed color, but it's virtually impossible to un-mix it to find exactly what the two original colors were.
