How This Works

DBSCAN finds dense groups of customers based on their behavior, marking outliers as anomalies.

  • Similarity Radius (ε): How similar customers must be (distance on the chart) to be considered neighbors.
  • Minimum Segment Size (MinPts): The minimum number of neighbors required to form a core customer segment.

The Process:

  1. It picks a customer to analyze (yellow).
  2. If they have enough neighbors within the radius, they become a core customer, starting a new segment. The algorithm then expands to find all connected customers.
  3. If not, they're marked as an anomaly (white), but could later be roped into a nearby segment.
  4. This repeats until all customers are analyzed.
