Support measures how frequently the genres in your rule are watched together across all users.

Calculate Confidence

Confidence measures how often the rule is true. Of all the users who watched the "if" genre, how many also watched the "then" genre?

Confidence(A → B) = (Users who watched both A and B) / (Users who watched A)

Calculate Lift

Lift tells us if there is a real association. Does watching Genre A actually increase the chances of a user watching Genre B?

Lift(A → B) = Support(A, B) / (Support(A) × Support(B))

Automated Mining: The Apriori Algorithm

Doing this manually is slow! Algorithms like Apriori find the best rules automatically by first finding all "frequent genre sets" that meet a minimum support threshold.

The Apriori Principle

"If a genre set is infrequent (e.g., very few people watch {Horror, Romance}), then any larger set containing it (e.g., {Horror, Romance, Comedy}) must also be infrequent."
