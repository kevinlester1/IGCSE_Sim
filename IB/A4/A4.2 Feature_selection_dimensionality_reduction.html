A4.2.2 Feature Selection

Identifying the most informative attributes.

Role: Feature selection is the process of choosing a subset of relevant features (variables, predictors) for use in model construction. Its main goal is to identify and retain the most informative attributes of the dataset while discarding redundant or irrelevant ones.

A4.2.3 Dimensionality Reduction

Transforming data to a lower-dimensional space.

Goal: Reduce the number of random variables under consideration by obtaining a set of principal variables. Unlike feature selection (which just drops columns), dimensionality reduction creates new combinations of attributes, preserving the relevant aspects (variance/information) of the original data in a smaller space (e.g., PCA).

The Curse of Dimensionality

As the number of features (dimensions) grows, the amount of data needed to generalize accurately grows exponentially. High dimensional spaces become sparse, making analysis difficult.
