Why AI Needs Practice: The Cycle of Training and Testing

Think about how you learn something new, like identifying different types of animals. At first, you might only know "dog" and "cat". If you see a horse, you might get confused. But after someone tells you, "That's a horse," you learn and get better.

Training an AI is very similar. It's not a one-time event; it's a cycle of learning, checking, and improving. This is called an iterative process.

Step 1: The First Training (The First Lesson)

In our simulation, the first thing we did was train the AI. We showed it pictures of red apples and yellow bananas. From this first lesson, the AI created a very simple set of rules in its "brain":

  • If it's Red and Round, it's an Apple.
  • If it's Yellow and Long, it's a Banana.

At this point, this is all the AI knows about the world. Its knowledge is very limited, based only on what we've shown it.

Step 2: The First Test (Checking its Homework)

Next, we tested the AI with images it had never seen before. This is the most important step because it reveals the weaknesses in the AI's training. In our simulation, the AI failed on several images:

  • The Green Apple (🍏): The AI was "Undecided".

    Why it Failed: The AI's rule for "Apple" only included the color Red. It had never been taught that apples could be green, so it didn't know how to classify it.

  • The Red Chili (🌶️): The AI guessed "Apple".

    Why it Failed: The AI saw the color Red and immediately thought "Apple". It got confused because the Long shape didn't match its "Apple" rule. It made a guess based on the strongest pattern it knew (Red = Apple).

  • The Robot (🤖): The AI made a wild guess.

    Why it Failed: The robot didn't match any of its rules. The AI had no category for things that are "Not a Fruit".

Step 3: The Iteration (Learning from Mistakes)

The failures in the test are not a problem—they are the most valuable part of the process! Each mistake gives us a chance to make the AI smarter. This is iteration: using feedback to improve.

When we went to the "Retrain" tab, we did two things:

  1. We Fix Mistakes: We tell the AI, "That green, round thing was an Apple." This adds a new rule to its brain. Now it knows apples can be Red OR Green.
  2. We Add New Knowledge: We tell the AI, "That robot is something different." This creates a whole new category called "Not a Fruit", making the AI much more precise.

After retraining, the AI has a bigger, better brain. If we test it again on the same images, it will now get them right.

The Cycle Continues

This is the iterative cycle of machine learning:

Train
→
Test
→
Find Mistakes
→
Retrain & Improve

You repeat this cycle over and over. Each time, the AI's knowledge gets a little bigger and more accurate. It's why machine learning is so powerful. The AI isn't programmed with every rule at the start; it learns the rules over time through this cycle of practice and feedback.
