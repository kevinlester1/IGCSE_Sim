Syllabus Point A4.3.4: Unsupervised Clustering

Assessment Standard: Describe how clustering techniques in unsupervised learning are used to group data based on similarities in features, specifically referencing applications such as customer base segmentation using purchasing logs.

1

Unsupervised Learning Fundamentals

Unlike supervised learning (which relies on pre-labeled target outputs), unsupervised machine learning analyzes unlabeled datasets. The algorithm independently discovers natural patterns, structural groupings, or feature relationships without explicit human feedback or ground-truth answers.

  • Input: Dataset $X = \{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_N\}$ without labels $y$.
  • Objective: Maximize intra-cluster similarity, maximize inter-cluster distance.
  • Key Algorithms: $K$-Means, Hierarchical Clustering, DBSCAN.
2

Feature Space & Vectors

Each data point is represented as an $n$-dimensional feature vector in a mathematical space. Features are measurable numerical attributes of the item.

$$ \mathbf{x}_i = \begin{bmatrix} x_{i1} & x_{i2} & \dots & x_{in} \end{bmatrix}^T $$

In customer segmentation, features include: $x_1 = \text{Annual Spend (\$)}$, $x_2 = \text{Purchase Frequency}$, $x_3 = \text{Recency (Days)}$.

3

Distance Metrics & Similarity

Proximity in the feature space represents behavioral or attribute similarity. The standard distance metric used in $K$-Means is Euclidean Distance (straight-line spatial distance):

$$ d(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (p_i - q_i)^2} $$

Smaller distance $\rightarrow$ Higher feature similarity $\rightarrow$ Grouped into the same cluster.

4

Real-World Marketing Workflow

In Customer Relationship Management (CRM), raw transaction logs are converted into feature vectors. $K$-Means then clusters customers into automated marketing personas:

Loyal Champions

High Spend, High Frequency. Strategy: Exclusive VIP perks & early access.

High-Value Casuals

High Spend, Low Frequency. Strategy: Targeted re-engagement triggers.
