Calculate Support

Support measures how frequently the items in your rule appear together in all transactions. It tells us the popularity of the item combination.

Support(A → B) = (Transactions with both A and B) / (Total Transactions)

Calculate Confidence

Confidence measures how often the rule is true. When a customer buys the "if" item, how often do they also buy the "then" item?

Confidence(A → B) = (Transactions with both A and B) / (Transactions with A)

Calculate Lift

Lift tells us if the rule is actually useful. It compares the confidence of the rule to how popular the "then" item is overall. Does buying A actually increase the chances of buying B?

Lift(A → B) = Support(A, B) / (Support(A) × Support(B))

Automated Mining: The Apriori Algorithm

Doing this manually is slow! Algorithms like Apriori find the best rules automatically by first finding all "frequent itemsets" that meet a minimum support threshold.

The Apriori Principle

"If an itemset is infrequent (e.g., very few people buy {Tea, Croissant}), then any larger set containing it (e.g., {Tea, Croissant, Muffin}) must also be infrequent. This lets the algorithm work much faster."
