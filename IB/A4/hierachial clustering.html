What is Hierarchical Clustering?

This is a method to group similar items. This simulation uses the "bottom-up" (agglomerative) approach where each customer starts as its own cluster and the algorithm progressively merges the closest pairs.

Linkage Methods

This setting changes how the distance between two clusters is measured:

  • Single: The distance is the shortest possible line between any customer in one cluster and any customer in the other.
  • Complete: The distance is the longest possible line between any two customers in the respective clusters.
  • Average: The distance is the average of all possible lines between the customers in the two clusters.

Understanding the Chart

The chart visualizes customer data based on two key metrics:

  • Annual Income (Horizontal): Customers to the right have higher incomes.
  • Spending Score (Vertical): Customers positioned higher up are bigger spenders.

Customer Personas:

  • Top-Right: High income, high spending. (Target group)
  • Bottom-Right: High income, low spending. (Savers)
  • Top-Left: Low income, high spending.
  • Bottom-Left: Low income, low spending.

The algorithm groups customers based on their proximity on this chart, identifying key market segments.
