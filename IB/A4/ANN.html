Building an Artificial Neural Network

Interactive Concept Guide
Step 1: The Building Block

The Single Perceptron

A single perceptron is a Linear Classifier. It draws a straight line through data.

It works perfectly for simple problems: "If it's red, stop. If green, go." It can separate these easily.

Limit: It can only draw one straight line.
Can separate Red vs Green
Step 2: The Problem

The "XOR" Limitation

What if the data is mixed? Imagine a "Smart Home":

  • Home & Safe: No Alarm (Green)
  • Away & Safe: No Alarm (Green)
  • Home & Intruder: ALARM (Red)

A single straight line cannot separate the red dots from the green dots here. This stymied AI research for years!

Impossible with 1 line!
Step 3: The Solution

Adding a "Hidden Layer"

If one person can't decide, hire a committee! We add a Hidden Layer of perceptrons.

Each neuron in the hidden layer draws its own line. Then, the Output Neuron combines their decisions.

Math Magic: Combining two straight lines creates a corner. Combining many lines creates a Curve or a Shape.
4 Neurons = 4 Lines
Combined = A Box!
