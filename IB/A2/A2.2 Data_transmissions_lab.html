A2.3.1 IP & NAT

Network Address Translation (NAT) allows multiple devices on a private network to share a single Public IP address. This helps mitigate the exhaustion of IPv4 addresses and adds a layer of security.

IPv4 vs IPv6

IPv4: 32-bit (e.g., 192.168.1.5). ~4.3 billion addresses.

IPv6: 128-bit (e.g., 2001:0db8::ff00:42:8329). Practically infinite addresses.

Private vs Public

Private: Used inside LANs (e.g., 192.168.x.x, 10.x.x.x). Not routable on the internet.

Public: Globally unique, assigned by ISPs. Routable on the internet.

A2.3.3 Packet Switching

Data is segmented into packets, each with a routing header. Packets travel independently through switches and routers and are reassembled at the destination.

The Process

  1. Data is divided into equal chunks (Segmentation).
  2. Header added (Src IP, Dst IP, Sequence Number).
  3. Routers independently determine best path per packet.
  4. Packets may arrive out of order.
  5. Receiver reassembles using Sequence Numbers.
