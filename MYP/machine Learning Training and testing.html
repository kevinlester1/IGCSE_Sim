First, Let's Plan Our Data! 🤔

Before we train our computer, we need to think like a scientist. A good machine learning model needs good data.

We need to ask: Where will we get our examples (our data) from? Will we draw them ourselves? Ask our friends? Find them online? Planning this helps make sure our data is good and fair.

A Quick Warning: Beware of Bias! 👀

Sometimes, the data we collect can be unfair. This is called bias.

🐕

Imagine you teach a robot about "dogs" but only show it pictures of golden retrievers. When it sees a tiny chihuahua, it might get confused and say "That's not a dog!"

That's bias in action. A good model needs a variety of fair data.

Keep an eye out for bias in our emoji data!

Another Trap: Overfitting! 🤯

Another problem is when a model learns your data too well. This is called overfitting.

🧑‍🎓It's like a student memorizing the exact answers to the practice test but not learning the real subject. They'll do poorly on the final exam!

Overfitting happens when the model learns mistakes or "noise" in your data. It's important to have clean data.

We'll see this happen in our simulation!

Connecting to MYP Design Criterion D: Evaluating

Strand i: Design a testing method

When we decided to split our data into "Training" and "Testing" piles, we were designing a fair test. The Testing Data, which the model has never seen, generates data (the model's predictions) to measure the success of our solution.

Strand ii: Evaluate the success of the solution

The "Model Report Card" was our evaluation. We didn't just look at the accuracy score; we critically evaluated the solution by looking at which emojis it got wrong. This helped us understand its specific strengths and weaknesses against our goal.

Strand iii: Explain how the solution could be improved

You didn't just stop at the first result! You acted like a real designer by suggesting improvements:

  • Fixing Overfitting by cleaning the noisy data.
  • Fixing Bias by adding more varied examples.
  • Improving the Model's Structure by adding a new "Other" category.

This iterative cycle of testing and improving is the key to good design.

Strand iv: Explain the impact of the solution

By fixing these problems, you increased the model's positive impact. An unbiased, accurate model is useful and fair to its users. A biased or overfitted model could make unfair or wrong decisions, having a negative impact on people who rely on it.
