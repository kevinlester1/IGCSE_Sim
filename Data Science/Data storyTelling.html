Functions of
Data Visualisation

Core Concepts, Trends, and Relationships

Comparisons

Showing the differences or similarities between values.

  • Highlighting gaps in performance or magnitude.
  • Using Bar and Column charts for categorical ranking.

Distribution

Displaying frequency and how data is spread over an interval.

Frequency

How often a value occurs.

Grouping

Identifying clusters or "bins" of data points.

Data Over Time

Visualizing trends or changes throughout specific periods.

Relationships

Showing connections or correlations between variables.

Mapping how one factor influences another, often using scatter plots or network diagrams.

Location & Range

Location

Geographical representation of data across regions.

Range

The spread between upper and lower limits.

Variations

Measuring deviation on a standard scale.

Real World Trends

Real-world data is messy. It rarely follows a perfect line.

  • Trends can be overall or segmental.
  • Description: Increasing, Decreasing, Stable, or Volatile.

The Mechanics of Correlation

Positive

Both variables move together.

Negative

One rises, the other falls.

Zero

No discernible relationship.

Measured on a scale of -1 to 1.

Managing Outliers

Observations that do not fit the established pattern.

The Error

Data entry mistakes (e.g., 50 vs 500).

The Insight

An indicator of unusual, significant real-world events.

Conclusion

Mastering these functions allows us to tell better stories with data.
